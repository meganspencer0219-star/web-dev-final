How to Get Started with Volleyball

  1. Choose a position that fits you:
    • Setter – runs the offense and sets up hitters.
    • Outside hitter – all-around player who passes, attacks, and blocks.
    • Middle blocker – quick attacks and front-row defense.
    • Libero/DS – back-row specialist who keeps the ball off the floor.
  2. Learn the core skills:
    • Passing (forearm platform and overhead).
    • Serving (floater, topspin, or jump serve).
    • Hitting (approach, arm swing, and timing).
    • Blocking and reading the hitter.
  3. Practice game situations:
    • Six-on-six scrimmages.
    • Serve-receive rotations.
    • Free ball and transition drills.

Basic Rules of Volleyball

Volleyball is played between two teams of six players on the court at a time. Here are a few of the simple rules newer players should remember:

  • Each team is allowed up to three contacts to return the ball over the net.
  • A player cannot contact the ball twice in a row (blocking doesn’t count as a contact).
  • A rally begins with a serve and ends when the ball hits the floor, goes out of bounds, or a fault is called.
  • Most competitive matches are played best-of-five sets to 25 points (15 in the fifth), win by two.
  • Players must stay in rotational order until the server contacts the ball.
